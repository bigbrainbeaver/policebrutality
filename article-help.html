To: United Nations

Office of the Secretary-General.

New York, NY 10017

America

Honorable Secretary-General,

I am writing to you to bring your attention to my concern of the alarming problem of police violence. This problem doesnâ€™t belong to any country, but to the whole world. This issue intersects with the lines of human rights, social safety, and justice.

Police violence has consequences beyond victims and their families ; it erodes trust between communities and the government itself. In addition, it violates the rule of law and increases social injustice.

I call United Nations to make pronouncements on concrete actions regarding, among others:

  1. International standarts: E.G. guidelines regarding police conduct that prioritize human rights and de-escalation tactics.
  2. Monitoring and Reporting: An international independent structure should be introduced to monitor the cases of police violence and also ensure accountability by governments for violation of human rights.
  3. Training and Education: That all police officers be properly trained in human rights, conflict resolution, and community liaison.
  4. Strengthening Support to Victims: Free legal and psychological assistance, guaranteed, should be available for victims of police violence and their family members.

While the entire world is watching, the United Nations has the opportunity to take actions, ensuring bright and violence-free future. Thank you for underlining this urgent problem. I want the whole world to take serious actions against police violence and for the protection of human beings.
